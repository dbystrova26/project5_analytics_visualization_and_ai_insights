# Use case proposals — Pan-European aparthotel AI strategy

---

## Use case 1 — Dynamic pricing / revenue optimisation engine

### Business problem
Chleo's revenue managers set nightly rates using seasonal rules and gut feeling. They miss event-driven demand spikes (festivals, conferences, public holidays) and often underprice during high-demand windows or overprice during slow periods, leading to empty apartments.

### Proposed solution
A gradient boosting model (XGBoost or LightGBM) trained on historical booking data predicts the optimal nightly rate for each property on each future date. The model uses:
- Lead time (days until arrival)
- Day of week and month
- Local event calendar flags
- Competitor rate benchmarks
- Historical occupancy at similar lead times
- Market segment mix (leisure vs corporate)

The model outputs a recommended rate with a human-readable explanation: *"Recommended €195 because: 2-day lead time (historically high conversion window), Primavera Festival detected in Barcelona this weekend, competitor median is €210."*

### Why relevant for this company size
At 500 employees across 10+ cities, pricing is currently decentralised and inconsistent. A centralised pricing engine creates a single source of truth while still allowing city managers to override recommendations.

### Dataset alignment
Hotel Booking Reservation 2024 + Hotel Prices in Europe 2024 — both contain ADR, lead time, market segment, and date features needed for this model.

### Expected value
8–15% RevPAR uplift is industry-standard for dynamic pricing adoption. For a chain this size, that likely represents €500K–€2M additional annual revenue.

### Dashboard metric
RevPAR vs forecast gap, ADR by city, pricing heat map by day of week.

## Use case 2 — Cancellation & no-show predictor

### Business problem
High cancellation rates, especially from OTA bookings with free-cancellation policies, leave apartments empty with no time to rebook them. Each empty night is 100% lost revenue with fixed costs still running.

### Proposed solution
A binary classification model (logistic regression or random forest) predicts the probability of cancellation for each booking 72 hours before arrival. Bookings above a risk threshold trigger an automated retention workflow:
- Personalised email/SMS with a non-refundable upgrade offer
- Or an early check-in incentive to confirm commitment

### Why relevant for this company size
At this scale, even a 5% improvement in net cancellation rate on a portfolio of 500+ apartments across 10 cities has material impact. Manual monitoring at this volume is not feasible.

### Dataset alignment
Hotel Booking Reservation 2024 — contains `is_canceled` flag, `lead_time`, `market_segment`, `distribution_channel`, `deposit_type`, and `days_in_waiting_list` — all strong cancellation predictors.

### Expected value
If the chain has ~100 cancellations per week and retention offers recover 10% of them at an average ADR of €150, that is ~€78,000 per year recovered — from a model that costs almost nothing to run.

### Dashboard metric
Cancellation rate by channel (OTA vs direct), cancellation rate by lead time bucket, weekly cancellation trend.

## Use case 3 — Automated guest communication & upsell agent

### Business problem
Guest relations is the most headcount-intensive function in an aparthotel. Each guest needs: pre-arrival instructions, city guide, digital key instructions, check-in support, mid-stay check-in message, upsell offers (parking, late check-out, extended stay), and post-stay review request. Across 10 cities and hundreds of guests daily, this is not scalable manually.

### Proposed solution
An LLM-powered agent (built with LangChain) handles the full guest communication sequence, triggered by PMS events via n8n webhooks. The agent:
- Pulls guest name, property address, apartment number, and check-in time from the PMS
- Generates a personalised message using a structured template with LLM-filled personalisation
- Selects the appropriate upsell offer based on booking length (short stay → late check-out; long stay → mid-week discount on extension)
- Sends via WhatsApp Business API or email (SendGrid)
- Logs every message sent with timestamp, content, and delivery status to a Google Sheet for audit

All factual fields (address, times, prices) are pulled from the PMS, not generated by the LLM, to prevent hallucination.

### Why relevant for this company size
500 employees across 10 cities cannot maintain a large central guest comms team. Automation here directly improves margin without degrading guest experience — if done with proper guardrails.

### Dataset alignment
No direct dataset needed — this is an operational automation. The booking dataset supports the upsell logic design (e.g. which stay lengths correlate with extension requests).

### Expected value
If the automation handles 70% of guest communications currently done by staff, and each city has 2 FTE doing this work at €35K/year fully loaded cost, that is €490K/year in labour cost avoidable — or staff can be redeployed to higher-value activities.

### Dashboard metric
Upsell conversion rate, review score trend, message response rate, average response time.
